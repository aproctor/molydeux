Frequently Asked Questions

Are there any rules for this event?

Don't start working on your game before the event. Pick your game ideas from @petermolydeux's Twitter feed. Molydeux has lovingly currated some ideas which he feels would best suit the jam here, but you can pick any of them.

Is everyone just working from one idea or can each team pick an idea they like?

You pick whichever @petermolydeux tweet you like.

What is the schedule for Molyjam?

Friday, March 30th, there will be a meetup at your local venue, where you can meet with the other devs at the jam, hang out, have drinks, form up into teams, discuss some ideas. There may likely be some announcements at this time. Saturday through Sunday afternoon, everyone can make the games. Sunday night, April 1st, there will be a games festival at your local venue. All precise hours are TBD.

Where do I go? What time?

Check with your local Facebook invite where the venue will be and what time.

Do I have to spend my entire weekend at the local venue or can I work from home?

We encourage you to come to your local event on Friday, March 30th so that you can meet other participating devs, hang out, have drinks, form up into teams. You can work from home, if you wish, the rest of the time, but please do attend the fest on Sunday night, where you can show off your game and have a chance to check out what other teams have made. All precise hours are TBD.

I don't have any programming skills. Am I doomed?

There will likely be an awesome engineer at your local meetup you can totally team up with, but if by some miracle you cannot find one, artist-friendly game engines will be provided. Here are some for you to familiarize yourself with: Game Maker and Game Salad. It is recommended you check out Unity, as well.

Will there be a way to participate remotely?

We are presently working this out.

Will the event be live-streamed?

We are presently working this out.
